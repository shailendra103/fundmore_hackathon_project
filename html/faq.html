FAQ Frequently Asked Questions

What is FundMore?

FundMore is Malaysia’s pioneer and most trusted peer-to-peer (P2P) lending and web-based platform where people invest directly in for fixed returns, supporting the core of our economy and fostering the entrepreneurial culture of Malaysia.

Using information sourced from official sources and information providers, FundMore reviews all applications before businesses are allowed to list on the platform. Each business is identity checked and only businesses with at least 1 year of accounts filed with Registry of Companies or have been in trading operations for 2 years qualify.

Investors then select who they wish to invest in by submitting note subscription offers. Investors can spread their risks by investing across different projects. Embodying the spirit of access, an investor can start investing from RM$1,000.

With FundMore, real estate players can raise funds from the ‘crowd’, diversify their source of financing and become empowered to accept their target interest rates. On the other hand, investors gain access to opportunities with potentially more attractive yields and participate together with businesses in their journey for growth.

Who may list on FundMore?

Opportunity Providers (OPs) will have to be in at least one of the following three categories and meet the respective requirements: Category Requirement 1. Developer: OPs who register themselves as developers will have to provide a developer licence number (either from Malaysia or from the home country of the OPs). 2. Registered Property Agent, Broker and/or Realtor: OPs who register themselves as property agents, brokers and/or realtors will have to provide their agent licence number (either from Malaysia or from the home country of the OPs). 3. Land and/or Property Owner: OPs who register themselves as owners will have to show documentary proof of such ownership. This can be in the form of title deeds, title searches, certificates of title and any other conclusive evidence of ownership.

What is crowdfunding?

Crowdfunding is defined as "the practice of funding a project or venture by raising many small amounts of money from a large number of people, typically via the Internet". FundMore’s model of crowdfunding involves creating an interactive platform for the purpose of bringing together property investors to network with OPs to co-invest in real estate projects and also to share knowledge and expertise.

Is crowdfunding regulated by the Malaysian authorities?

FundMore has a license from the Malaysian Securities Commission to raise debt financing from investors of real estate projects.

How much real estate projects has been listed on the site?

Since our launch in, more than RM$10 million worth of real estate projects have been listed by FundMore.

Do you develop and put your own projects on FundMore?

No. FundMore is not a property development company. We do not solicit any funds for our own projects. To maintain neutrality, FundMore does not invest in any of the listed property projects as well.

How do you charge for your services?

We do not charge consumers for using our site. We charge OPs the following services: 1. Premium Advertising on FundMore; 2. Membership fees. 3. Crowdfunding service fees When OPs are keen to engage our services, we will provide an estimated fee package. This quote is derived from our projection of the scope of work required based on the OPs’ targeted project value. It is not pegged to the actual project value after it is successfully concluded.

Do you provide advice on the projects listed on FundMore?

While we provide a transparent Risk Assessment Score for investors to review. We do not provide any advice on the projects that investors should fund. It is entirely at the discretion of investors and they are encouraged to seek professional advice if in doubt.

Can FundMore refund the money if the project does not materialize?

No. As the collaboration in relation to projects are directly between the OPs and investors, any such discussion should take place between the OPs and the investors. In relation to P2P Lending Projects, FundMore has the authority to act on behalf of the investors to remedy any payment defaults, hire third party professional debt collectors to claim unpaid amounts and commence legal proceedings.

How does FundMore show the completion of projects?

FundMore require the OPs to submit and show the status of the projects development on a quarterly update basis. This status update will be provided in the Investors’ Portfolio page when the investor logs in.

Will FundMore manage my money?

No. FundMore does not collect any funds or deposits. Any collaboration in relation to projects are directly between the OPs and investors. We do not act as agent for either the OPs or the investors.

Are the monies guaranteed or protected?

No.
